Lateral entry in Haryana: how it works

The Lateral Entry Entrance Test (LEET) is how a diploma holder in Haryana turns a diploma into an engineering degree — joining a B.Tech / B.E. degree directly in the second year instead of starting over in the first.

In Haryana, lateral entry into B.Tech is handled through the state's technical-education admission process (an entrance test and/or merit-based counselling). The exact exam name, dates and eligibility change year to year, so confirm the current official notification before applying.

Heads-up: exam names, dates, fees and cut-offs for Haryana change every year. OneLeet focuses on preparation; for application dates and seat details, always check the official Haryana notification.

Eligibility at a glance

Most Haryana aspirants qualify with a three-year engineering diploma and the minimum marks the state specifies. Reserved categories usually get a relaxation, and some states admit B.Sc (with Maths) candidates too. Read the full LEET eligibility guide.

Frequently asked questions

How does lateral entry work in Haryana?

A diploma holder applies for lateral-entry seats and, based on an entrance test and/or merit, is allotted a seat directly in the second year of B.Tech at a participating Haryana college. The process is run by the state's technical-education authority. Confirm current specifics in the official notification.

Who is eligible for LEET in Haryana?

Typically a three-year engineering diploma with the minimum marks set by the state (often around 45–50%, relaxed for reserved categories); some states also allow B.Sc holders with Mathematics. See our eligibility guide and verify the Haryana notification.

What should I study for lateral entry in Haryana?

Focus on Mathematics first, then Physics, Chemistry and core engineering. Practise with past papers and mock tests on OneLeet.
